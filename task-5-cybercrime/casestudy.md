# Case Study: Identity Theft

Identity theft is a cybercrime in which a person’s personal information, such as their name, bank details, Aadhaar number, or login credentials, is stolen and misused without their consent. This information is often used for financial fraud, creating fake accounts, or unauthorized transactions.

A common way identity theft happens is through phishing or fake websites. For example, a college student named Rahul received a message claiming to be from his bank, asking him to verify his account details through a link. The link led to a fake website that looked exactly like the bank’s official page. Trusting it, Rahul entered his login ID, password, and OTP. Within minutes, the attacker accessed his bank account and transferred money to another account. In some cases, stolen data is also used to create fake social media profiles or apply for loans.

Identity theft usually targets students, working professionals, and elderly people who may not always verify the authenticity of online messages. Students are especially vulnerable because they frequently use multiple online platforms and may unknowingly share personal information.

The consequences of identity theft can be serious, including financial loss, damage to one’s reputation, and emotional stress. Victims may also face difficulty recovering their accounts or proving their identity. This case highlights the importance of staying cautious online, avoiding suspicious links, and never sharing sensitive information like passwords or OTPs with anyone.
